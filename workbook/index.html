Philosophy Workbook Table of Contents

This is very much a work in progress. I have barely started with laying out the website, though I have 90,000 words of content.

Since the structure is evolving fairly quickly, I am not building fancy navigation for now but keeping this table of contents in one place. I'll sprinkle breadcrumbs and other aids once I go a few weeks without restructuring.

The top levels are intended (for now) to be complete but I find I am changing them rather often. I have lower level content in Scrivener, the tool I use for writing anything more than a few pages, unless it needs live code embedding in which case, RStudio Notebooks. However, it needs a another draft before I amd ready to publish here.

Major headings in black aren't intended to have content, leaf levels that have content have links in grey that you can click, others will be completed as soon as I can. Start with Why read this? to get some sense of what this is about and where it is going.
